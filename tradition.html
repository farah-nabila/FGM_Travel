Source: Google

Kadazandusun people

Kadazandusun also known as Dusun or Kadazan a term embracing a number of peoples that together constitute the largest indigenous ethnic group in the state of Sabah, Malaysia, on the north eastern extremity of the island of Borneo. This community speaks in Kadazan or Dusun. Kadazan society is based on patrilineal descent groups; marriage within a descent group is generally forbidden. Most Kadazan are Christian, although there also is a significant Muslim community. Small groups maintain local religions in which priestesses conduct a variety of agricultural and communal rituals.

Malay People

There also have Melayu community which is not as much as in West Malaysia that live in Sabah. This community speaks in Malay and conforms to Malay customs.

Bajau People

This community is a second largest community in Sabah and there also have this community in Ranau, Sabah. Bajau community also known as Sama. This community had been split into two main groups which is sedentary agriculturists of the north coast and seafaring people of the east coast. Their languages, which are related to those of the southern Philippines, are not all mutually intelligible.

CUSTOMS

Source: Google

Kadazandusun People

Kadazandusun community Their ancient beliefs on the verity that everything has life - the rocks, trees, and rivers are all living things. This community has their unique customs which every of them will celebrate it every year. The customs that they celebrate which is Pesta Kaamatan or Harvest Festival. This celebration to honour the Rice Spirit - Bambaazon or Bambarayon and giving thanks for yet another bountiful year. The festival begins on the first of May at many district levels. The rites and customs of the Pesta Kaamatan is a tribal practice of Kadazandusun and also Murut peoples.

Malay People

Malay people have some customs which is according to Islamic religious. Malay people celebrate few important events which is “Hari Raya Aidilfitri” and “Hari Raya Aidiladha”. Other than that, Malay people also celebrate “Maulud Nabi” also known as “Maulidur Rasul”. According to Islamic calendar, Malay had been celebrating this festival as written in the Qur'an. During this festival, it makes a relationship between each other become closer.

Bajau People

Bajau people had celebrating a festival of Regatta Lepa known as Sea Gypsies that celebrating by Bajau Laut people. The activities that they celebrate includes a floating parade of colourful lepa boats, lepa boat racing and tug-of-war, beauty pageant and fireworks. Held annually in Semporna since 1994, the Regatta Lepa water festival is one of the longest-running and most unique celebrations of its kind in Malaysia and Borneo.
